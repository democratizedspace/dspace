Electronics quests teach circuit fundamentals: power, signal flow, measurement, and safe
prototyping. You will build small systems that support robotics, sensors, and energy work.

## What you will practice

- **Circuit basics**: LEDs, resistors, and safe current limiting.
- **Measurement**: checking voltage, continuity, and battery health.
- **Microcontrollers**: Arduino setup, logging data, and sensor wiring.
- **Documentation**: wiring diagrams and test logs for repeatability.

## Quest flow highlights

Electronics quests start with blinking LEDs and basic circuits, then progress to Arduino
projects like temperature logging and voltage checks. Later tasks validate cable continuity
and battery pack health.

## Inventory highlights

- Arduino Uno boards, solderless breadboards, LEDs, resistors, and jumper wires.
- 9 V batteries, USB cables, thermistors, and ultrasonic sensors.
- Servo test rigs and indicator modules for rapid prototyping.

## Why it matters

Electronics is the backbone of automation. It connects power systems to sensors, robots,
rocket avionics, and monitoring infrastructure.
