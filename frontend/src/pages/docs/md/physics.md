Basic physics explains how matter and energy behave.

Newton's first law states that objects resist changes in motion.
This inertia shows up in many systems, from sliding coins to spinning wheels.

For a solid cylinder, the moment of inertia is
$$I = \tfrac{1}{2} m r^2$$
where $m$ is mass and $r$ is radius.
Understanding inertia helps estimate stored energy and plan experiments.
