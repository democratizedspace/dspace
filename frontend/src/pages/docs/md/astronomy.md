Astronomy quests teach observational skills for the night sky, from locating constellations to
capturing long-exposure star trails. The focus is on repeatable observing routines rather than
one-off sightings.

## What you'll practice

- **Sky navigation** — identify Polaris, major constellations, and seasonal star hops.
- **Planetary observing** — track Jupiter's moons, Saturn's rings, and Venus phases.
- **Event tracking** — monitor meteor showers, lunar eclipses, and ISS flyovers.
- **Astrophotography basics** — plan and capture star trail stacks.

## Inventory and tools to expect

Astronomy gear in the inventory includes:

- Basic refracting telescopes and planisphere star charts
- Camera rigs for star trail stacking
- Observation logs for tracking conditions

## Observation tips

- Let your eyes adapt to the dark for 15–20 minutes before judging visibility.
- Record sky conditions every session; it makes comparisons meaningful.
