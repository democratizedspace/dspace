3D printing in DSPACE focuses on reliable, repeatable fabrication with entry-level FDM gear. The
quests walk you through calibration, maintenance, and practical parts so you can ship useful
objects, not just test cubes.

## What this skill covers

- Dialing in first layers, temperature, and retraction for clean prints.
- Maintaining a printer (nozzle swaps, belt tension, filament changes).
- Printing functional parts like cable clips, spool holders, and phone stands.
- Using small calibration prints to validate setup before long jobs.

## Recommended inventory

- Entry-level FDM printer, nozzles, and PLA filament for daily printing.
- Print tools like a scraper, calipers, and a spool of filament for test runs.
- Calibration prints such as a Benchy or temperature tower sample for quick checks.

## Quest highlights

- [Start 3D printing](/quests/3dprinting/start) and [Bed leveling](/quests/3dprinting/bed-leveling)
  establish a reliable first layer.
- [Calibration cube](/quests/3dprinting/calibration-cube) and
  [Temperature tower](/quests/3dprinting/temperature-tower) tune accuracy and heat.
- [Retraction test](/quests/3dprinting/retraction-test) reduces stringing on real parts.
- [Phone stand](/quests/3dprinting/phone-stand) is the first functional print milestone.

## Best practices

1. Run a quick calibration print before any multi-hour job.
2. Keep the build plate clean and re-check Z-offset after nozzle changes.
3. Store filament dry and label spools by material and tuned temperature.
4. Document any changes in the quest log so future prints stay predictable.
