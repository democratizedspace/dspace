3D printing in DSPACE is all about reliable FDM fabrication: calibrate the machine, tune for
repeatable parts, and learn how to recover from print failures so you can keep shipping hardware.

## What you'll practice

- **First-print readiness** — square the frame, level the bed, and confirm your first layer.
- **Calibration workflows** — run a calibration cube, temperature tower, and retraction tests to
  lock in your baseline.
- **Everyday prints** — output useful parts like phone stands, spool holders, and cable clips.
- **Troubleshooting** — diagnose clogs or blob-of-death failures and bring the printer back online.

## Inventory and tools to expect

The inventory focuses on entry-level FDM rigs and consumables so you can keep iterating quickly:

- Entry-level FDM printer variants (leveled bed, clean nozzle, or belt tensioned)
- PLA filament by weight for calibration prints
- Replacement brass nozzles and sample Benchy prints for baseline comparisons

## Pro tips

- If first-layer adhesion drifts, re-run bed leveling before touching slicer settings.
- Keep a log of temperature tower results; it makes material swaps faster.
- Treat nozzle clogs as a normal maintenance cycle, not a failure.
