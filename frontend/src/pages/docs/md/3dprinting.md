3D printing in DSPACE focuses on practical, repeatable fabrication. You will learn to tune an
entry-level FDM printer, troubleshoot failures, and ship useful parts you can reuse in other
skill trees.

## What you will practice

- **Calibration and tuning**: bed leveling, retraction tests, temperature towers, and
  calibration cubes to dial in print quality.
- **Failure recovery**: clearing clogs, cleaning nozzles, and recovering from a blob of death.
- **Functional parts**: printing phone stands, cable clips, spool holders, and rocket parts.
- **Material discipline**: tracking filament usage with dPrint and keeping spools organized.

## Quest flow highlights

The 3D printing questline starts with bed leveling and first-layer checks, then pushes through
benchy runs for repeatability. Mid-tier quests focus on retraction, temperature, and nozzle
maintenance. The later quests move into useful parts like phone stands, cable clips, and
spool holders, plus the larger milestone of printing large batches for consistency.

## Inventory highlights

- Entry-level FDM printers with leveled beds and clean nozzles.
- PLA filament (white/green), replacement nozzles, and print removal scrapers.
- Calibration artifacts like Benchies and temperature towers for quality baselines.
- Functional prints including phone stands, cable clips, and filament spool holders.

## Why it matters

Reliable fabrication unlocks the rest of the world. When your printer is tuned, you can
fabricate rocket components, generator housings, robotics brackets, and replacement tools
on demand. 3D printing is the bridge between digital design and physical capability.
