A compact home lab lets you extract useful compounds, tune water chemistry, and practice safe
reactions with small-batch equipment.

## Core tools

- **Hot plate** – provides steady heat for boiling or evaporation
- **Glassware** – beakers, stir rods, funnels, and filter paper for separation
- **Scale** – measure ingredients accurately
- **Safety gear** – nitrile gloves, goggles, and a cleanup plan

## What you'll practice

- **pH control** — test, adjust, and buffer solutions for hydroponics or aquaria.
- **Dilution math** — safely work with acids and bases.
- **Extractions** — turn harvested plants into usable compounds (like stevia).
- **Reaction safety** — document procedures and stop if temperature runs away.

## Inventory and tools to expect

The inventory includes lab essentials like glass beakers, nitrile gloves, and safety goggles.

## Safety tips

1. Wear eye protection and gloves when heating or pouring chemicals.
2. Work in a well-ventilated area away from flames.
3. Keep a fire extinguisher and first aid kit nearby.
