It's been a while! Time for a DSPACE update! v3 is here.

What's DSPACE, you ask? You must be new around here, and if so, welcome! I'm glad you're here. DSPACE is a space exploration game that I've been working on for a while now. It's still in its early stages, but I'm excited to share it with you. You can learn more about the game on the [About](/docs/about) page.

-   Added a persistent dark mode toggle to the site header, letting players switch themes without losing their preference on reload.

## Custom Quests

You can now create your own custom quests on‑device for whatever you want! The game's source code contains the set of all official quests (which generally in some way relate to space exploration, however loosely), and anything on your device is considered non‑canon. However, if you choose to contribute to the base game, there's also a procedure to make your quests canon by submitting a pull request. The in‑game UI helps you do this without touching a single line of code or opening a text editor. Just follow the [Quest Submission Guide](/docs/quest-submission).

Quest art now stays on your machine, too—the quest editor converts uploaded images into data URLs,
keeping previews and saved custom quests completely local even before you share them.

## Custom Items & Processes

Not only can you create custom quests, but v3 also introduces the ability to create your own items and processes! Want to design a new type of rocket fuel? Create a custom manufacturing process? Now you can! The game includes:

-   A visual editor for creating new items at [/items/create](/items/create)
-   A process designer for creating custom manufacturing chains at [/processes/create](/processes/create)
-   Built‑in validation to ensure your creations will work
-   The ability to preview and test before finalizing
-   **NEW**: Unified bundle submission workflow at [/bundles/submit](/bundles/submit) that keeps related quests, items, and processes together in a single pull request

Like custom quests, your creations are stored locally by default. When you're ready to contribute, package everything into a bundle following the [Custom Content Bundles](/docs/custom-bundles) format and submit it through the in-game interface. This ensures related content stays synchronized and makes reviews more efficient.

## Streamlined Game Systems

We've simplified DSPACE's core mechanics to focus purely on what matters most: the joy of space exploration and the real‑world impact of your achievements. The game now operates with a cleaner, more direct connection between virtual progress and real‑world space advancement.

Our virtual units (dWatt, dUSD, etc.) remain as intuitive measurements of your progress, but they're now more tightly integrated with tangible outcomes. This change emerged from community feedback and our commitment to maintaining a clear path between in‑game achievements and real‑world space development.

## Local‑First Architecture

We've rebuilt DSPACE's architecture to be truly local‑first. Your progress, achievements, and contributions are now stored primarily on your own device. This approach gives you complete control over your data.

A few technical details that might interest you:

-   Your v2 progress will automatically migrate to v3. On first launch, v3 checks for the legacy
    `gameState` key in `localStorage`. If found, it copies the data into IndexedDB and removes the
    old keys.
-   All custom content includes rollback functionality (in case something goes wrong)

The new **Cloud Sync** feature lets you back up your progress across devices using a private
GitHub gist. Visit the [Cloud Sync](/docs/cloud-sync) page to enable it.

## Refined Virtual Economy

We've removed all blockchain integration plans from DSPACE. While Web3 features were originally planned, we realized they could potentially harm our core mission of democratizing space exploration. The virtual units (dWatt, dUSD, etc.) work perfectly well as simple progress metrics without needing tokenization.

**Coming in future updates:** For social features and cross‑instance communication, we're planning to implement ActivityPub. This will give us all the benefits of decentralized social networking without the downsides of blockchain integration. Your achievements, collaborations, and contributions will be shareable and verifiable across instances, just in a simpler and more reliable way.

## AI Integration Updates

Instead of relying on commercial LLM providers like OpenAI, DSPACE now uses our own distributed computing project [token.place](https://token.place). This allows us to use idle resources at home (and other people's homes, eventually) to fulfill the LLM needs of non‑business‑critical applications such as DSPACE. Both DSPACE and token.place are intended to be fully runnable on cheap home hardware, and are therefore both fully open source and permissively licensed.

### dChat knowledge upgrade

dChat now ships with an in-game knowledge base that summarizes canonical quests, items, processes, and the player's local inventory snapshot. This gives the assistant immediate context to answer gameplay questions without waiting for future updates or external documentation lookups.

## Community Features

**Coming in future updates:** Enhanced social features focused on direct collaboration between players. You'll be able to share your achievements, help others with their space missions, and contribute to collective goals – all without any intermediary systems. This will create a more immediate and meaningful connection between players working toward common space exploration objectives.

## Self‑hosted

Starting with DSPACE 2.1, the website is self‑hosted on premises, instead of deploying via Netlify. It's currently running on an M4 Mac Mini. Redundancy is here.

## Switching to MIT License

While 0BSD sounds like a nice license, it is in some cases not on the list of accepted OSS licenses on the Internet. Therefore, I'm switching from 0BSD to MIT, which is still very permissive but much better supported.

## Looking Forward

These changes reflect our commitment to keeping DSPACE focused on its core mission: democratizing space exploration through education, simulation, and real‑world impact. By removing unnecessary complexity, we've made the game more accessible while strengthening the connection between virtual achievements and actual space development.

Thank you for being part of this journey. The future of space exploration belongs to everyone, and DSPACE will continue evolving to make that future more accessible to all.
